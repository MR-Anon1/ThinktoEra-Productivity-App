Privacy at a glance. ThinktoEra uses Firebase Authentication and Cloud Firestore to provide accounts and cloud synchronization. It does not use Firebase Analytics or Firebase Crashlytics, does not collect location data, contains no advertising SDKs, and currently offers no purchases or subscriptions. Some focus-session and notification information remains on your device.

For purposes of applicable data-protection law, Poorab Khawale is responsible for the personal information processed through the App, except where a third-party provider acts independently under its own terms and privacy policy.

3. How we use information

We use information only as reasonably necessary to:

  • create, authenticate, maintain, and secure your account;
  • save, retrieve, organize, and synchronize your productivity content;
  • provide planner, task, project, review, focus, settings, export, and account-deletion features;
  • schedule and display reminders you request;
  • maintain app reliability, diagnose failures, prevent abuse, and protect users and services;
  • respond to support, privacy, and legal requests; and
  • comply with applicable law and enforce applicable terms.

Depending on your location, processing may be based on performing the service you request, your consent, compliance with legal obligations, or legitimate interests such as security and reliability where those interests are not overridden by your rights.

We do not sell personal information and do not use your productivity content for targeted advertising.

4. Google Sign-In and Firebase Authentication

The App uses Google Sign-In as an authentication option and Firebase Authentication to establish and maintain your account session. When you use Google Sign-In, Google may provide Firebase and the App with basic account information such as your unique account identifier, email address, display name, and profile photo URL. ThinktoEra does not receive your Google password.

Your interaction with Google is also governed by the Google Privacy Policy. Firebase Authentication maintains the account information needed to identify you and associate your cloud data with your account.

5. Cloud Firestore and cross-device synchronization

For authenticated users, Cloud Firestore stores tasks, projects, daily planner data, planner templates, weekly reviews, and user settings beneath an account-specific user identifier. Firestore-backed information can synchronize between devices when you sign in with the same account.

Firestore may maintain an offline cache on your device to support responsive access and synchronization. Changes made while connectivity is unavailable may be transmitted when connectivity returns.

Access is restricted using Firebase Authentication and Firestore Security Rules designed so authenticated users can access only data associated with their own user ID. Google describes Firebase privacy and security practices, including encryption in transit and encryption at rest for Cloud Firestore and Firebase Authentication, in its Firebase Privacy and Security documentation.

6. Local storage and notifications

Local storage

Focus sessions and certain notification or preference information are stored locally on your device and may not synchronize between devices. Local information can remain on a device after you sign out. You can generally remove it by using available in-app controls, clearing the App's local data through your operating system, or uninstalling the App.

Local notifications

The App uses local notifications for reminders. Notification schedules and related settings are processed on your device. The App may request operating-system notification permission. You may disable notifications through the App's settings, where available, or through your device's system settings.

Notification text may be visible on your lock screen depending on your operating-system preferences. Review your device notification-preview settings if other people can access your device.

7. Optional Sentry error reporting

Firebase Crashlytics is not used. A distributed build may optionally enable Sentry only when the developer configures a Sentry DSN. When enabled, Sentry may receive technical diagnostics such as an error message, stack trace, affected operation, app version, platform information, and associated timestamps. The App configures Sentry not to send default personally identifiable information and does not enable performance tracing; however, technical reports can sometimes contain information present in an error context.

Diagnostic information is used to identify and correct crashes, errors, security problems, and reliability issues. Sentry processes information under its own Privacy Policy. If Sentry is not configured in a build, the App does not transmit reports to Sentry.

8. Information and services not used

In the current version:

  • Firebase Analytics is not used;
  • Firebase Crashlytics is not used;
  • precise or approximate device location is not collected;
  • contacts, microphone recordings, camera content, and browsing history are not collected by ThinktoEra;
  • there are no advertisements or third-party advertising SDKs, including AdMob; and
  • there are no in-app purchases, paid subscriptions, or payment processing features.

If these practices change in a future version, this policy will be updated and any legally required notice or consent will be provided.

Third-party services operate under their own terms and privacy policies. We encourage you to review them.

10. Security

ThinktoEra uses reasonable technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the nature of the information, including Firebase Authentication, account-scoped Firestore Security Rules, HTTPS communication, and platform security controls. Firebase reports that Cloud Firestore and Firebase Authentication encrypt data in transit and at rest.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. You are responsible for protecting access to your Google account, device, and operating-system profile. Notify us if you believe your ThinktoEra account or information has been compromised.

11. Data retention and deletion

Cloud account and productivity information is generally retained while your account remains active or as needed to provide the App. It may also be retained where reasonably necessary to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, prevent abuse, enforce agreements, or maintain security.

The App provides account controls that allow you to export your Firestore-backed user data and delete your account. Account deletion is designed to delete the associated Firestore collections and Firebase Authentication account. Copies may remain temporarily in provider backups, security records, or logs until overwritten or deleted under the applicable provider's retention cycle.

Local focus or preference data may remain on a particular device unless removed through in-app controls, operating-system data controls, or uninstalling the App. Optional Sentry reports, if enabled, are retained according to the configured Sentry project settings and legal or security requirements.

12. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to request access, correction, export, deletion, restriction, or objection concerning your personal information, and to withdraw consent where processing relies on consent. You may also have the right to complain to your local data-protection authority.

  • Access and export: use the data-export option in the Account area or contact us.
  • Correction: update available profile information and edit your productivity content in the App.
  • Deletion: use the account-deletion option or contact us for assistance.
  • Notifications: change reminder settings in the App or operating system.
  • Google account access: manage connected-app access through your Google Account settings.
  • Local information: clear application data or uninstall the App.

We may need to verify your identity before completing a privacy request. We will respond within the period required by applicable law. These rights may be subject to lawful exceptions.
